The Purpose:


 The Portfolio is created as a time capsule for my projects between 2016 and 2020. They marked my beginnings in computer science and my flaws. One notable trait is reckless disregard of copyrights. As a high school student, I have a tendency to use "Google" images without citing sources. In the summer of 2020, I remove the worst abuses, but minor ones are kept for record. Due to the obscurity of these projects, I highly doubt that I will be sued for copyrights.



 Projects are separated into three categories: project, codePen, and p5 sketches. Each one represents the medium from which it was produced. Moreover, the projects are organized chronologically by publish date. You are welcome to examine and copy the code if you desire to. I will be happy if it is of use somehow.




Exhibition:


Ca-Ro


Ca-Ro is an independent project made in the conclusion of 10th grade. It is a unique variation for Tic-Tac-Toe connoisseur and is often play in Vietnam. The objective is to be the first to get 5-in-a-row. Set in endless mode, the entire grid is fair game! Best of tactics and enjoy!

2Bit Soccer


2Bit Soccer is a fun 2-players game made in 11th grade to simulate concepts learned in physics class, namely kinematics and collision. The objective is to kick a ball into the opponent's goal. The first to 7 is the victor.

Type-o-meter


Type-o-meter is an independent project made in 12th grade as a part of the AP Computer Science Principles exam. Simply put, it guages the user's typing speed. This is done by calculating the user's words typed to time elapsed ratio as the user replicates APIs generated sentences.
