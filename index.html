The Purpose:


 The Portfolio is created to house my creations between the year 2016 to 2020. They are separated into three categories: project, codePen, and p5 sketches. Each one represents the medium from which it was produced. The projects are organized chronologically by publish date not revision date, so it is possible for one to be unnaturally superior to others in the proximity.



 You are welcome to examine and copy the code if you desire to, but don't forget to exercise proper citations! Plagiarizing is a heavy crime. I will suggest that you only copy the code that you understand; otherwise, it may have portentous side effects.




Exhibition:


Ca-Ro


Ca-Ro is an independent project made in the conclusion of 10th grade. It is a unique variation for Tic-Tac-Toe connoisseur and is often play in Vietnam. The objective is to be the first to get 5-in-a-row. Set in endless mode, the entire grid is fair game! Best of tactics and enjoy!

Mission: Defense


Mission: Defense is a projectile-based game made in 11th grade. The idea was inspired from lessons gleaned in physic class. Although the mechanics behind it was original, it must be known that Pokemon belongs to Nintendo. As for the game instructions, they may be accessed in the console.

The Quest of Orpheus


The Quest of Orpheus is a text-adventure game made in 11th grade. Its short and concise plot is modeled off of the mythologies of ancient Greece; mainly, Orpheus's. Although the game is short and sweet, it boasts a fun combat system and memorable texts. The best way to know is to try.
